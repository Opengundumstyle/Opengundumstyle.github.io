Why software engineer ?

“It is far more common for people to allow ego to stand in the way of learning.” ― Ray Dalio, Principles

For a period of time in my life, I lost motivation on learning. Being lost and depressed, I developed a plan to make a significant change - forcing myself to do difficult things or things I perceived as hard to accomplish. I started to read, to work out, to learn coding in a daily basis. Despite being constantly challenged by the hardship of such endeavor, I slowly started to enjoy the process of exploring, struggling, adopting and understanding. It was when I realized that learning a new thing is not as hard as it seems as long as I put my mind to it. And whatever that's viewed as difficult will eventaully gets easy with consistency of deliberate practice.

What made me decide to switch:

The shift of paradigm has made me decide to persuit software engineering. I wanted to take my coding skill to another level and enabling myself to contribute my passion toward coding for a bigger purpose. So, I decided to join App Academy.

What I learnt during the bootcamp:

During the several months of grinding and building projects, I've gained the mindset of trying out things in different ways, and does not afraid of messing up. As a software engineer, I've came to the conviction that nothing is going to work accordingly. However it's crutial to embrace each failure and learn from it. Then seek for a better apporach.

Take the leap of faith for every single attempt, just like recursion :p

Being a software engineer helped straighten my thought process, it makes me a better decision maker. It reignited my desire to learn, to not care if I look stupid during the quest of building something I am not familiar with. It encouraged me to communicate, to listen, to exchange ideas. Most importantly, to think rationally toward "bugs" and look for the practical solutions when obstacle presents itself.
